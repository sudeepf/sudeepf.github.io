Hello!


I am,

Graduate Student in Computer Vision at, Carnegie Mellon University and

Capstone Member at Amazon Lab126


My major contributions and interests
are in application oriented extensions of:
  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Computer Vision
  • Deep Learning
  • Robotics
  • Image Processing
  • Computational Perception
  • Vision-based Human Machine Interaction.

Last summer, I did my internship with Uber Advanced Technologies Group in Pittsburgh, where I ideated, prototyped, developed, evaluated and then deployed a real-time scene understanding module for Uber's current self-driving vehicle fleet. These module played a very critical role in extending the capabilities of autonomous vehicles. Before attending Carnegie Mellon, I was a research engineer at Uurmi systems, where in conjunction with my undergraduate lab(RRC) and led a team of 22 people developing in-house low-cost self-driving car technology. There I spent my time in developing end-to-end system architecture and real-time modules for stereo reconstruction, segmentation, prediction, a parallel framework for mapping, visual odometry, Kalman fusion for localization, and global and local path planning. I also worked on embedded vision projects such as real-time stereo camera calibration and rectification and embedded vision on FPGAs and low-power processors. At Carnegie Mellon, I have been working with Dr. Deva and Dr. Katerina on 3D Human pose estimation from a monocular image (sponsored by Amazon Lab126). This involves an end-to-end deep learning method to estimate pose in volumetric representation.
